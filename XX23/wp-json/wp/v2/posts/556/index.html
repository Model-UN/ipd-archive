The U.S. Senate has officially passed many resolutions regarding the Cuban Missile Crisis, including plans regarding the U.S. Presidential Cabinet. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

With this, one of the papers not passed was the RULE paper, sponsored in part by Republican Sen. James Pearson from Kansas. This paper essentially planned on making sure that the executive branch would inform Congress of any military action to keep Congress updated and they would be able to move forward or not with the actions after 30 days. Sen. Pearson was disheartened and shared his sentiments on this matter by expressing how he felt strongly regarding the resolutions passed, as well as his resolution.\u00a0<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In an interview with Sen. Pearson, he expressed how \u201cthe cabinet has not been informing the senate of practically anything that has been going on with Cuba.\u201d  <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Regarding the failure of his paper he has stated the reason behind this is due to \u201ca few people who were very much like \u2018this is too much bureaucracy, this is raising tensions between the executive and legislative branch.'” <\/p>\n\n\n\n

He further explained how the reasons for not passing his paper seem hypocritical due to his fellow senators having a similar resolution where the president would be in power for no more than 30 days with the military to protect the country.\u00a0<\/p>\n\n\n\n

He finished by explaining how \u201cthis is just quite frankly ridiculous that the senate passed this because again, it just increases tensions between the executive and legislative branch\u201d and how on day 31 of a conflict, \u201cwe are out of luck.\u201d\u00a0 Even after coming up with solutions, they do not seem to be solving the issue at hand, but rather creating possible internal resentment within the senate.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
